How treats benefits your rabbit's health

While it's essential to provide rabbits with a balanced diet primarily consisting of hay, fresh vegetables, and pellets, occasional treats can offer several benefits. Firstly, treats like small pieces of fruit or herbs can serve as a positive reinforcement during training sessions, making it easier to bond with and train your rabbit.

Benefits of Functional Training for your Bunny

Functional training for rabbits offers numerous benefits for their overall health and well-being. It helps improve their agility, balance, and coordination, enhancing their ability to navigate their environment safely. By incorporating functional exercises like tunnel running, obstacle courses, and hopping challenges, rabbits can maintain a healthy weight and reduce the risk of obesity, which is common in sedentary indoor rabbits.

Wet-Food and Dry food for teh health of your Bunnies

Both wet and dry food have their merits when it comes to feeding rabbits. Wet food, such as fresh vegetables and fruits, provides essential hydration and a variety of nutrients, promoting overall health and helping prevent dehydration. Dry food, like high-quality pellets, offers a convenient and balanced source of essential nutrients, including fiber, which aids in dental health and proper digestion.

THE 7 ESSENTIAL STEPS

  1. Suitable Enclosure
    Ensure your rabbit has a spacious and secure enclosure.
  2. Proper Diet
    Balanced diet of hay, fresh vegetables, and rabbit pellets.
  3. Regular Grooming
    Do regular grooming to prevent matting and hairballs.
  4. Social Interaction
    Spend time with your rabbit, petting and playing to keep them active.
  5. Healthcare
    Schedule regular check-ups with a veterinarian of small animals.
  6. Safe Environment
    Rabbit-proof your home to prevent accidents.
